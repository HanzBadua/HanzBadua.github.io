Introduction

Space colonization, in short, is the concept (or long-term goal) of establishing a permanent and self-sustaining human presence/inhabitation outside of Earth. The presumed location of these futuristic colonies are usually the Moon, Mars, or even in orbit.  Space colonization as a concept was not only designed for science fiction, but for legitimate purposes as well, such as the continuation and survival, and even progress of the human race. Space colonization also continues to exist as a legitimate long-term goal of varying space programs.

Lastly, many design and engineering groups at NASA have determined that a "scientific breakthrough of the century" will not be required to start staging basic colonies, although the sheer amount of engineering, resources, and human effort required is not achievable in the near future.
